The goal of this study was to examine the combined effects of gender stereotypes in job advertisements and AI-based recruitment tools’ recommendations on hiring decisions. Thus, the job advertisements as well as the candidate profiles you were asked to evaluate were altered to test these effects.

We asked you to select a candidate to be hired using a specialized digital hiring platform where you are presented with a job advertisement and condensed candidate profiles. The platform aided in making hiring decisions by highlighting important words and phrases in both texts. For some participants, an AI-based recruitment tool and a diversity cue recommended a candidate. Although what you saw was designed by researchers for the purpose of the present study, similar automated and AI-based hiring tools are rapidly becoming a mainstay of hiring and recruitment (HR) processes. More companies are adopting these technologies, with IBM finding a 19% usage in IT firms.

As these tools become part of hiring processes, the public has an interest in learning the tools’ effects on HR practices and hiring decision-making. Indeed, the use of these tools in HR can have both positive and negative effects. However, due to their novelty and potential to impact economic opportunities, researchers must disentangle the tools’ influences and aid informed, measured responses in light of social and legal implications. We thank you for helping further research in this area.
